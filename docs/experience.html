Experience

Select roles spanning marine research, cross-cultural development, scientific communication, and laboratory research.

Research Intern, Davis Strait Expedition

Greenland Institute of Natural Resources

Operated aboard the RV Neil Armstrong in a high-stakes, physically demanding offshore environment sampling zooplankton and identifying fish larvae to understand Arctic species distribution patterns and potential climate-driven shifts in marine ecosystems. The work demanded extreme attention to detail under pressure: executing precise biological sampling in harsh conditions, maintaining rigorous protocols despite weather and sea state, and seamlessly integrating into a multidisciplinary scientific crew. This fieldwork contributed to ongoing Arctic ecosystem research, published in the University of Washington Arctic Research Technical Report TR-2305.

weltwärts Development Volunteer

Partnership Rhineland-Palatinate / Rwanda, Kigali

Worked across the social sector in partnership with local networks serving people living with HIV, widows, and people with disabilities. This role required active listening, collaborative project coordination across diverse stakeholders, and the ability to adapt strategies to local contexts and resources. It deepened my understanding of how technical solutions must be grounded in the social, economic, and cultural realities of the communities they serve.
